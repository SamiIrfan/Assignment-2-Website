Monthly Newsletter

News 1

The Amazon River begins as capillaries of water trickling down the eastern slopes of the Andes. The waters flow, gathering sediments, nutrients and organic material, braiding into a vast hydrologic system that stretches over 4,000 miles across 40% of the South American continent – the equivalent of a river that stretched from Los Angeles to New York City and back again.


News 2

The Amazon rainforest harbors the highest biodiversity on Earth, functioning as a vital carbon sink that filters greenhouse gases. Thousands of tree species and unique wildlife rely on this complex ecosystem, which produces 20% of the world’s oxygen. However, alarming deforestation rates are endangering this delicate balance, threatening indigenous communities.


News 3

Rapid glacier retreat in the Andes Mountains is disrupting the Amazon’s water sources, threatening the fresh water supply of millions of people downstream. As temperatures rise, critical Andean snowpack shrinks, reducing the tributaries that feed the basin’s massive flow. Scientists warn that these changing hydrological rhythms could permanently destabilize the tropical rainforest ecosystem.
